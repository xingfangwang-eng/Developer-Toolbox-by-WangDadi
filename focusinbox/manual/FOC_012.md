# focusinbox - FOC_012

## Overview

This is the architectural specification for focusinbox, a zero-latency email triage engine engineered for high-volume inbox environments. It leverages deterministic priority scoring and real-time threading to eliminate cognitive overhead in email management.

## Detailed Content

### What is focusinbox?

focusinbox is an AI-native email organization system that ingests IMAP streams, applies a multi-dimensional urgency matrix (based on sender reputation, semantic urgency, and temporal decay), and surfaces a single actionable queue. It operates on a sub-100ms processing pipeline per message, ensuring that your inbox is always pre-sorted before you open it.

### Core Features

- **Adaptive Priority Scoring Engine**: Each inbound email is evaluated against a weighted composite of 12 parameters: `sender_trust_score` (derived from historical reply latency and domain authority), `content_urgency_signal` (NLP-based detection of keywords like "deadline," "ASAP," "critical"), and `thread_heat_index` (recency and frequency of replies within a conversation chain). The final score is calculated via `P = (w1 * S) + (w2 * U) - (w3 * D)`, where `D` is a logarithmic decay factor over 72 hours. Results are computed in under 50ms per message via a Rust-based core engine.

- **Real-Time Thread Decomposition & Clustering**: Instead of flat threading, focusinbox performs micro-clustering on multi-party conversations using a custom `conversation_id` hashing algorithm that maps `Message-ID`, `References`, and `In-Reply-To` headers into a DAG (Directed Acyclic Graph). This enables sub-10ms traversal of complex email chains, even with 500+ messages in a single thread. The system exposes a `thread_depth` and `unresolved_count` metric for each cluster, allowing users to instantly identify stalled conversations.

- **Predictive Snooze with Temporal Context Awareness**: The snooze function is not a static timer. It leverages a `temporal_relevance_model` that re-evaluates snoozed emails against your calendar (via CalDAV integration) and upcoming deadlines. For example, an email snoozed until "next Tuesday" will automatically resurface 2 hours before a related calendar event. The system maintains a `snooze_resurrection_queue` in PostgreSQL, indexed by `epoch_resurface_time` and `user_id`, ensuring sub-millisecond retrieval during peak hours.

## How to Use

1. **Initial IMAP Configuration & Trust Profile Seeding**:  
   Connect your email account by providing IMAP server credentials (e.g., `imap.gmail.com:993`). The system will perform a one-time scan of your last 90 days of sent/replied emails to build a `sender_trust_profile`. This profile is stored as a JSONB blob in PostgreSQL, keyed by `sender_email` and `domain`. Run `focusinbox init --imap-server imap.gmail.com --port 993 --ssl` to trigger the scan. The process typically completes within 2 minutes per 10,000 emails.

2. **Tuning the Priority Matrix via CLI**:  
   Adjust the weight coefficients directly from the command line to match your workflow. For example, to increase the importance of sender reputation over keyword urgency, execute:  
   `focusinbox config set priority.weights.sender_trust=0.6 priority.weights.urgency=0.3 priority.weights.decay=0.1`  
   The system will immediately recalculate all unread messages and update the queue. You can verify the new distribution with `focusinbox stats --priority-histogram`.

3. **Creating a Custom Snooze Rule with Calendar Binding**:  
   To link a specific email to a calendar event, use the `--calendar-bind` flag. For instance:  
   `focusinbox snooze --email-id 0x1A2B3C --until "2025-05-20T14:00:00Z" --calendar-bind "Project Review"`  
   This command parses your CalDAV feed for an event titled "Project Review" on that date, and registers the email in the `snooze_resurrection_queue` with a `pre_event_wakeup_seconds` of 7200 (2 hours). The email will resurface exactly 2 hours before the event, regardless of your current inbox state.

## Best Practices

- **Practice 1: Weekly Trust Profile Refresh**  
  Schedule a weekly cron job to run `focusinbox profile refresh --period 7d`. This re-evaluates sender trust scores based on your recent interaction patterns. For example, if a previously low-priority sender suddenly becomes a daily collaborator, their `sender_trust_score` will be automatically adjusted upward. This prevents stale prioritization in dynamic team environments.

- **Practice 2: Use Thread Depth Thresholds for Batch Archival**  
  Configure an automated archival rule for threads with `thread_depth > 50` and `unresolved_count = 0`. Execute:  
  `focusinbox archive --rule "thread_depth > 50 AND unresolved_count == 0" --action move-to-archive`  
  This keeps your active inbox focused on actionable conversations while maintaining full searchability in the archive. It is especially effective for high-volume mailing lists or automated notification threads.
