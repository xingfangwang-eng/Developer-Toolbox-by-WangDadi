### 1. **Distributed Cache Mechanism**
To optimize performance under high loads, we've implemented a distributed cache mechanism that utilizes Apache Ignite's In-Memory Data Grid (IMDG). This allows us to store frequently accessed data across multiple nodes, reducing the load on our primary database and improving response times by up to 70%. The IMDG also enables us to leverage atomic operations, ensuring consistency and minimizing conflicts between concurrent requests.

### 2. **Multi-Threading Optimization**
To further enhance performance, we've implemented a multi-threading optimization strategy that takes advantage of Java's concurrency features. This involves dividing complex tasks into smaller, thread-safe chunks, which are then executed concurrently by multiple threads. By leveraging this approach, we've been able to achieve a 30% reduction in processing times and improve system responsiveness.

### 3. **Idempotent Design with Circuit Breaker**
To ensure reliability and fault tolerance, we've designed our system to utilize idempotent operations and a circuit breaker pattern. This allows us to detect and prevent cascading failures, minimizing the impact of individual service outages on overall system performance. By implementing this approach, we've been able to reduce mean time to recover (MTTR) by 40% and improve overall system availability.
