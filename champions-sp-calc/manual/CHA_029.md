# champions-sp-calc - CHA_029

## Overview

**champions-sp-calc** is a precision-engineered SaaS tool designed for competitive intelligence analysts, growth hackers, and product managers who need to reverse-engineer market positioning and calculate strategic parity scores. This isn’t another generic calculator—it’s a domain-specific engine that maps your product’s feature vectors against competitor benchmarks using weighted statistical models. Optimized for **English-speaking SaaS teams**, this tool eliminates guesswork from competitive analysis and delivers actionable parity metrics in seconds.

## Core Features

### 1. Weighted Feature Parity Engine (WFPE)
Unlike simplistic comparison tables, champions-sp-calc implements a **multi-attribute utility model** that lets you assign custom weights to each feature dimension (e.g., performance, UX, pricing, integrations). The engine normalizes raw data across competitors and computes a **Parity Score (0–100)** per category, plus an aggregate **Strategic Alignment Index**. This enables you to identify exactly where your product under-indexes or over-delivers relative to market leaders.

### 2. Dynamic Gap & Opportunity Heatmap
The tool automatically generates a **color-coded heatmap** that visualizes feature gaps across up to 10 competitors simultaneously. Each cell reflects the normalized difference between your product’s score and the market median. Hover over any cell to reveal the **delta percentage** and a **recommended action** (e.g., “Invest in scalability—your score is 23% below median”). This feature is powered by a custom **z-score normalization pipeline** that accounts for variance in competitor data quality.

### 3. Scenario Simulation with Monte Carlo Sensitivity
Go beyond static analysis. champions-sp-calc includes a **Monte Carlo simulation module** that runs 10,000 iterations to predict how changes in your feature weights or competitor scores affect your overall parity ranking. You can adjust sliders for “Price Sensitivity” or “Feature Velocity” and see real-time confidence intervals for your Strategic Alignment Index. This is critical for **roadmap prioritization** and **investor pitch preparation**.

## How to Use

### Step 1: Define Your Competitive Vector Space
Start by creating a new project in the dashboard. Use the **Vector Editor** to add up to 15 feature dimensions (e.g., “API Latency,” “Onboarding UX,” “Support SLA”). For each dimension, assign a **weight (0–1)** based on its strategic importance to your target market. Then, input your own product’s scores (1–10) for each dimension. The tool auto-saves your vector space as a JSON schema, which you can export for team collaboration.

### Step 2: Import or Manually Enter Competitor Data
Use the **Bulk Import** feature to upload competitor data via CSV or direct API from market intelligence platforms (e.g., G2, Capterra). Alternatively, manually enter scores for up to 10 competitors per dimension. The system validates data consistency and flags outliers (e.g., a score of 10 for “Price” when the median is 3). Once imported, the **Parity Engine** computes z-scores and generates a preliminary **Competitive Profile Matrix**.

### Step 3: Run Simulation and Export Actionable Insights
Navigate to the **Scenario Lab** tab. Adjust the “Feature Velocity” slider to simulate a 15% improvement in your product’s “API Latency” score. Click “Run Monte Carlo” to see how your Strategic Alignment Index shifts from 64 to 79 with a 95% confidence interval of ±3.2. Finally, click **Export Report** to generate a PDF containing the heatmap, parity scores, and simulation results—ready for your next product review meeting.

## Best Practices

### Practice 1: Normalize Data Before First Use
Competitor data from public sources is often noisy—some reviewers inflate scores, others under-report. Before running any analysis, use the **Data Sanity Check** feature within champions-sp-calc to apply a **trimmed mean filter** (remove top and bottom 5% of scores per dimension). This prevents outliers from skewing your parity scores. For teams with mature data pipelines, integrate the tool’s REST API to pull scores directly from your own CRM or product analytics stack.

### Practice 2: Run Scenario Simulations Before Major Roadmap Decisions
Don’t rely on static snapshots. Before committing to a feature build (e.g., “Add multi-tenant support”), simulate how a +2 score improvement in that dimension affects your overall parity ranking. Use the **Monte Carlo Sensitivity** to test worst-case, median, and best-case scenarios. If the simulation shows a <5% improvement in Strategic Alignment Index, deprioritize that feature in favor of one that moves the needle more (e.g., “Reduce onboarding time”). This data-driven approach aligns engineering effort with competitive impact.
