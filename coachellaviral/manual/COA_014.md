## What is coachellaviral?

coachellaviral is a **real-time content orchestration engine** that automates the creation, distribution, and amplification of Coachella-themed viral assets. It hooks into social APIs, trend signals, and user-generated content to generate high-engagement posts before, during, and after the festival. Think of it as a **growth hacking toolkit** for music festival marketing.

## Core Features

### 1. Real-Time Trend Injection Engine
Automatically scrapes emerging hashtags, memes, and audio clips from TikTok, Instagram, and Reddit during peak festival hours. It then dynamically injects these into your content pipeline, ensuring your posts are always riding the wave of the current viral moment—not lagging behind by 6 hours.

### 2. Multi-Platform Asset Generator
Generates platform-optimized assets (Reels, Shorts, TikTok videos, and static carousels) from a single input. It handles aspect ratios, caption length, and hashtag density per platform. No more manual cropping or rewriting—just one prompt, and it spits out a ready-to-publish bundle.

### 3. Viral Score Predictor & A/B Scheduler
Uses a lightweight ML model trained on past Coachella viral posts to predict the *Viral Probability Score* (VPS) of any asset before it goes live. It then automatically schedules the top-scoring variants to publish during the highest engagement windows (e.g., 30 minutes before a headliner set).

## How to Use

### Step 1: Configure Your Viral Trigger Signals
Set up your campaign by defining **trigger signals**—these are the events, keywords, or audio clips that will activate your content pipeline. For example: `"When 'Bad Bunny' trends on X with >10k mentions in 5 minutes, generate a meme template with the caption: 'Me waiting for Bad Bunny to bring out a surprise guest.'"` Use the `coachellaviral config` CLI to define these rules in a YAML file.

```yaml
triggers:
  - keyword: "Bad Bunny"
    platform: "X"
    threshold: 10000
    window: 5m
    action: generate_meme
    caption_template: "Me waiting for {artist} to bring out a surprise guest"
```

### Step 2: Run a Dry Campaign Simulation
Before going live, run a **dry simulation** using historical Coachella data. This will show you how your content would have performed in past years. Use the command:
```bash
coachellaviral simulate --campaign coachella2024 --mode dry
```
This outputs a report showing predicted VPS scores, best posting times, and potential reach. Adjust your triggers based on the simulation feedback.

### Step 3: Deploy the Viral Loop
Once satisfied, deploy the campaign in **live mode**:
```bash
coachellaviral deploy --campaign coachella2024 --mode live
```
The engine will now monitor real-time signals, generate assets, and auto-schedule posts. Use the dashboard to monitor VPS scores in real-time and manually override any low-performing posts.

## Best Practices

### 1. Use the "Pre-Festival Tease" Loop
Start your campaign 7 days before Coachella. Use the engine to generate **countdown posts** and **speculative memes** about the lineup. This builds anticipation and trains the algorithm to recognize your account as a *primary source* for festival content. By Day 1 of the festival, your account will have higher organic reach.

### 2. Always Have a "Contingency Asset" for Technical Failures
During live events, network congestion and API rate limits can cause delays. Pre-generate a set of **fallback assets** (e.g., "We’ll be right back after this technical pause" memes) and store them in the engine’s cache. If a trigger fails to fire, the fallback asset automatically posts to keep your audience engaged while you debug.
