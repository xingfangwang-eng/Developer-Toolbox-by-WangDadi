# coachellaviral - COA_002

## Overview

**coachellaviral** is a SaaS-native, AI-driven viral content engine purpose-built for music festival marketing — specifically engineered for Coachella-level brand amplification. It transforms raw festival assets (audio snippets, visual loops, crowd energy clips) into algorithm-optimized, platform-native viral assets across TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts, and X. This isn’t a generic scheduler. It’s a predictive virality layer for festival marketers, talent managers, and brand strategists who need to dominate the cultural conversation during Coachella season.

## Core Features

### 1. Predictive Virality Engine (PVE)
Real-time trend injection across 12+ music festival datasets. PVE analyzes TikTok trending audio, Instagram Reel tempo patterns, and YouTube Shorts retention curves to auto-sync your content with what’s about to pop — not what already peaked. It cross-references Coachella’s official lineup drop, stage schedules, and influencer activity to generate content sequences with 89% forecasted engagement lift.

### 2. Multimodal Asset Splicer (MAS)
One input, 7 platform-ready outputs. Upload a 30-second crowd clip or a backstage B-roll. MAS auto-extracts audio peaks, visual motion vectors, and color grading metadata, then remixes them into platform-optimized formats: 9:16 for Reels, vertical 4:5 for TikTok, square for X, and horizontal 16:9 for YouTube. Each output is pre-tagged with Coachella-specific hashtags, geo-location metadata, and trending sound overlays.

### 3. Viral Cascade Scheduler (VCS)
Time-release virality orchestration. VCS doesn’t just schedule posts — it simulates viral cascades. It staggers content drops across time zones (PST, EST, GMT, JST) based on historical Coachella engagement curves, and auto-retargets underperforming assets to alternative platforms with optimized captions and CTA variants. It also triggers cross-platform repost loops when a post hits a 10% engagement threshold within 30 minutes.

## How to Use

### Step 1: Inject Raw Festival Data into the Predictive Virality Engine
Navigate to the `Assets Dashboard` → `Upload Media` → `Bulk Import`. Drag-and-drop your raw footage (MP4, MOV, HEIC up to 4K). Enable `Auto-Trend Scan` — this triggers PVE to cross-reference your content against the current Coachella trending pool (e.g., “Doja Cat surprise set” or “Desert X installation”). The engine returns a `Virality Score` per asset (0–100). Only assets scoring above 75 proceed to the next stage. Use the `Audition Mode` to preview trend-matched audio overlays before finalizing.

### Step 2: Configure Multimodal Outputs via the Asset Splicer
Select your high-scoring assets → click `Splice to All Platforms`. The MAS interface shows a side-by-side preview of each platform variant. Adjust the `Motion Intensity` slider (1–10) to control clip speed and zoom dynamics. For TikTok, set `Hook Duration` to 2.5 seconds (optimal retention). For Instagram Reels, enable `Color Grade Sync` to match Coachella’s official palette (saturated warm tones, high contrast). Each variant auto-generates a `Caption Matrix` with 3 platform-specific copy options. Confirm with `Export All`.

### Step 3: Deploy the Viral Cascade Scheduler
Go to `Campaigns` → `New Cascade`. Paste the exported asset URLs from Step 2. Set `Cascade Type` to `Time-Zone Staggered` and select `Coachella Weekend 1` as the event anchor. VCS auto-calculates optimal drop times: Day 1 assets at 10:00 AM PST (pre-festival hype), Day 2 at 2:00 PM PST (peak crowd energy), Day 3 at 8:00 PM PST (post-headliner virality). Enable `Auto-Repost` with a 15-minute delay if initial engagement drops below 5%. Click `Deploy Cascade`. Monitor real-time engagement via the `Viral Heatmap` — VCS will auto-retarget underperforming posts to alternative platforms after 60 minutes.

## Best Practices

### 1. Pre-Load the Engine with Historical Data
Don’t start from scratch. Import at least 50 previous Coachella viral posts (from your own campaigns or competitor analysis) into PVE before the festival. This trains the engine on your specific brand’s audience patterns. Set a `Baseline Virality Threshold` of 70 — anything below will waste your content calendar. For maximum accuracy, update the dataset 72 hours before the lineup announcement.

### 2. Use the Cascade Scheduler for Post-Festival Echo Loops
Most marketers stop after the festival. Don’t. Schedule a secondary cascade 48 hours after the final Coachella weekend. VCS will auto-pull UGC from the event (crowd reactions, artist interactions, desert sunsets) and remix them into “Best of Coachella 20XX” compilations. These perform 3x better in the 7-day post-event window because they capture the “FOMO recovery” audience. Set the `Echo Loop` toggle to `On` in the VCS settings.
