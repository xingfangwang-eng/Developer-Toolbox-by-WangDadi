# coachellaviral - COA_008

## Overview

coachellaviral is a high-performance viral content engine engineered specifically for Coachella and major music festival marketing. It leverages real-time trend scraping, cross-platform audio-video synchronization, and predictive virality scoring to help developers, growth hackers, and marketing teams generate and deploy festival content that actually spreads. Designed for the US market, it cuts through the noise of generic social media tools by targeting the unique temporal and cultural dynamics of music festival audiences.

## Core Features

### 1. Real-Time Festival Trend Sync Engine
Automatically ingests live data from Reddit, TikTok, Twitter/X, and YouTube Music API during Coachella weekends. The engine identifies trending audio clips, dance challenges, and meme formats within minutes of emergence, then generates optimized metadata (captions, hashtags, timestamps) for your content pipeline. No manual monitoring, no lag.

### 2. Predictive Virality Scoring (PVS)
A proprietary machine learning model trained on 10,000+ festival viral posts from 2022–2024. PVS assigns a 0–100 score to any draft content based on audio engagement decay, visual density, and platform-specific timing windows. You get a clear “Go / Pivot / Kill” recommendation before you hit publish.

### 3. Cross-Platform Audio-Video Chain Builder
Automatically stitches together user-generated clips, official Coachella live streams, and trending sounds into a single, platform-optimized asset. Supports vertical 9:16 for TikTok/Reels, square 1:1 for Instagram, and 16:9 for YouTube Shorts. Outputs ready-to-upload files with embedded captions and dynamic CTA overlays.

## How to Use

### 1. Deploy the Trend Sync Agent
```bash
pip install coachellaviral-cli
coachellaviral init --project myfestival2025
coachellaviral sync --sources tiktok,reddit --keywords "Coachella 2025 viral, Sahara tent crowd, Do Lab remix"
```
This spins up a local daemon that polls every 90 seconds during festival hours. Results are cached in a local SQLite db and pushed to your webhook endpoint (e.g., a Slack bot or a Notion database). Use `--export json` for direct integration into your CI/CD pipeline.

### 2. Score Draft Content with PVS
```python
from coachellaviral import PVS

scorer = PVS(api_key="your_key")
result = scorer.evaluate(
    audio_file="sahara_drop.wav",
    video_file="crowd_reaction.mp4",
    platform="tiktok",
    publish_window="2025-04-18T20:00:00Z"
)
print(result.score)  # e.g., 87.3
print(result.recommendation)  # "Go"
```
The PVS model factors in audio decay (how fast a sound is losing traction), visual complexity (crowd density, lighting), and timing (publishing 2 hours before peak mobile usage). Use this to filter out low-potential content before it wastes your production budget.

### 3. Build and Export a Cross-Platform Asset
```bash
coachellaviral build \
  --clips crowd.mp4,stage_wide.mp3 \
  --overlay trending_hashtags.json \
  --format tiktok,reels,shorts \
  --output ./export/festival_day2/
```
This command auto-crops, syncs audio to video, and injects a dynamic CTA (e.g., “Tag your squad for Day 3”). Outputs are timestamped and named by platform. For headless deployments, use `--webhook https://your-cdn.com/upload` to push directly to your S3 or Cloudflare R2 bucket.

## Best Practices

### 1. Time Your Publishing to the “Second Peak”
Most festival content gets posted during the event (8 PM–2 AM) or the next morning. But the second peak—12 PM–2 PM the following day—has 40% higher engagement because users are recovering and scrolling. Use coachellaviral’s `publish_window` parameter to schedule your PVS-approved assets for that window. This is a known behavioral gap that most marketers miss.

### 2. Use Audio Decay as a Kill Switch
A trending sound at 9 PM might be dead by 11 PM. Configure your Trend Sync Agent to automatically downgrade any PVS score below 60 if the audio’s decay rate exceeds 0.3 per hour. This prevents you from wasting production resources on a fading meme. Set `--decay_threshold 0.3` in your sync config. It’s brutal but necessary for high-volume festival campaigns.
