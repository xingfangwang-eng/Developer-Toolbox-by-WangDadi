## Core Features

### 1. Sentiment‑Driven Content Cascade
coachellaviral continuously scans Twitter, Reddit, TikTok, and Discord for festival‑specific mentions. When a spike in positive or negative sentiment is detected (e.g., “Beyoncé just showed up”), the tool automatically **re‑prioritizes** your content queue, pushes a pre‑rendered reaction video, and posts a follow‑up poll or thread—all within 90 seconds. No manual triggers. No missed moments.

### 2. Multi‑Modal Asset Generator
Stop editing the same video for three platforms. coachellaviral takes a single raw clip (e.g., a 30‑second crowd shot) and **programmatically creates** a TikTok‑optimized 9:16 version with auto‑captions, a YouTube Shorts variant with end‑screen cards, and an Instagram Reel with trending audio overlay. It even rewrites the caption tone per platform—snappy for TikTok, story‑driven for IG, link‑focused for X.

### 3. Predictive Virality Scoring
Every piece of content receives a **Virality Index (VI)** score (0–100) before it’s published. This score is derived from historical Coachella content performance, current hashtag velocity, artist popularity decay curves, and time‑to‑event proximity. If a post scores below 70, coachellaviral **blocks it** and suggests a rewrite or a different visual asset. This eliminates guesswork and raises your floor for organic reach.

## How to Use coachellaviral

### Step 1: Ingest Festival Data & Set Viral Parameters
Start by connecting your Coachella API key (or manually entering the lineup, stage schedule, and location). Then define your **viral threshold**—minimum engagement rate per platform and maximum response latency. For example, set `engagement_rate_min = 4.5%` and `latency_max = 120s`. coachellaviral will use these as guardrails for all automated decisions. Run `./coachellaviral init` to pull the latest artist bios, setlist histories, and audience demographics.

### Step 2: Build & Schedule a Content Matrix
Use the dashboard to create a **content matrix**—a grid of assets x platforms x time slots. Upload raw assets (video, image, audio) and tag them with artist names, stage locations, and emotional intent (e.g., hype, nostalgia, surprise). coachellaviral will auto‑generate variants for each platform and assign a VI score. Review the matrix, adjust any low‑scoring assets, then hit **Deploy**. The tool will queue everything based on predicted peak attention windows (e.g., 10 PM PST on Saturday night).

### Step 3: Monitor & Tweak in Real Time
During the festival, open the **Live Dashboard**. You’ll see a real‑time feed of published content, engagement graphs, and sentiment deltas. If a post underperforms (e.g., VI drops below 60 after 15 minutes), coachellaviral will suggest a **content swap**—replace the asset with a higher‑scoring alternative from your matrix. You can also manually trigger a cascade: click “Force Cascade” to push a reaction video to all platforms simultaneously. All changes are logged for post‑festival analysis.

## Best Practices

### 1. Pre‑Load a “Crisis & Surprise” Content Pack
Coachella is chaotic. Before the event, prepare a **crisis pack** (e.g., “Artist Cancels,” “Technical Failure,” “Weather Alert”) and a **surprise pack** (e.g., “Unexpected Guest,” “Stage Dive,” “Record‑Breaking Moment”). Use coachellaviral’s `--pack-import` flag to load these as fallback assets. When a real‑time event triggers a sentiment shift, the tool will auto‑select the most appropriate pack and deploy it. This prevents you from scrambling for a response while the moment passes.

### 2. Use A/B Testing for Caption Tone
Don’t assume one caption style works for all. In the content matrix, create two variants for each asset: one with a **high‑urgency tone** (e.g., “YOU WON’T BELIEVE WHO JUST WALKED ON STAGE”) and one with a **community‑driven tone** (e.g., “The crowd is losing it right now. Are you watching?”). coachellaviral will automatically A/B test the first 100 impressions on each platform and then lock in the winning variant. This is a proven way to lift CTR by 12–18% on festival content.
