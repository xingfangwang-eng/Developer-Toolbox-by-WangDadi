## Executive Summary

coachellaviral is an AI-driven, real-time content amplification engine purpose-built for music festival marketers, artist managers, and brand strategists targeting the Coachella ecosystem. Unlike generic social media schedulers, it leverages festival-specific sentiment signals, geotagged engagement data, and audio-visual fingerprinting to predict and engineer viral moments before they peak. ## Hardcore Features

### 1. Real-Time Sonic Signature Detection & Remix Trigger
The engine ingests live audio streams from 12+ festival stage feeds and third-party UGC sources. Using a custom-trained CNN-LSTM model on Coachella’s historical setlists and crowd reaction patterns, it identifies “viral-ready” sonic signatures (e.g., a drop, a guest appearance, a crowd singalong). Instantly, it auto-generates a short-form video template (Reel/Shorts) with the detected audio, overlays trending text captions from the festival’s Twitter firehose, and pushes it to a queue for manual approval or auto-publish. This reduces the time from “moment happens” to “content goes live” to under 90 seconds.

### 2. Geofenced Sentiment Heatmap + Influencer Clustering
Instead of relying on broad hashtag volume, coachellaviral builds a 3D heatmap of the Empire Polo Club grounds by triangulating geotagged posts, Bluetooth beacon data, and Instagram Story location stickers. It then applies a graph-based clustering algorithm to identify micro-influencers physically present at high-sentiment zones. The tool allows you to drop a “virtual pin” on the map and instantly get a ranked list of accounts with the highest engagement-to-follower ratio within that 50-meter radius. You can then trigger a direct DM campaign with a pre-approved pitch template, all from the same interface.

### 3. Predictive Virality Score (PVS) with Auto-Budget Reallocation
Every piece of content you schedule or approve gets a PVS from 0–100, calculated from 17 factors: time-to-sunset, competing artist set times, current festival crowd density, and historical CTR for similar visual styles (e.g., “sunset silhouette”, “confetti canon”). If a post’s PVS jumps above 85 within 30 minutes of going live, the system automatically reallocates your ad budget from underperforming static posts into a “boost” campaign for that high-potential asset, targeting users within a 20-mile radius of the festival or those who have interacted with Coachella-related content in the last 48 hours.

## Step-by-Step Operational Guide

### Step 1: Configure the Audio Fingerprint Vault
Before the festival gates open, navigate to the `Sonic Signatures` tab. Upload the official setlist or SoundCloud previews for the headliners you’re tracking. The system will perform a spectral analysis to create a unique fingerprint for each track segment. Set your “Trigger Threshold” to `0.82` (a float value representing the confidence level required for a match). Then, define your `Content Templates`—for example, map a “crowd singalong” fingerprint to a template that includes a “Lyrics on Screen” overlay and a call-to-action sticker linking to your artist’s merch store. This ensures that when the audio is detected, the system doesn’t just record it—it builds a ready-to-publish asset tailored to that specific moment.

### Step 2: Deploy the Geofence & Activate the Sentiment Grid
Use the `Map View` to draw a precise polygon around the festival grounds. Enable the `High-Frequency Polling` toggle (set to 15-second intervals) to capture rapid sentiment shifts. In the `Influencer Cluster` panel, set the `Minimum Engagement Radius` to `30 meters` and the `Follower Ceiling` to `50,000`. This filters out mega-influencers and focuses on high-intent micro-accounts. Run the initial scan; the system will return a JSON payload of candidate accounts. Review the `Authenticity Score` (a composite of comment sentiment and posting frequency) and batch-select the top 10. The system will auto-generate a personalized DM from a template you provide, inserting the user’s name and the name of the stage they are near.

### Step 3: Set Up the Auto-Budget Cascade
In the `Budget` section, link your ad platform API (Meta, TikTok, or Google Ads). Define a `PVS Floor` of `75`—any content scoring below this will not trigger auto-boosting. Create a `Cascade Rule`: if PVS >= 85, allocate 30% of your remaining daily budget to a “Reach” campaign targeting `[geo: 20-mile radius around Indio, CA]` and `[interest: Coachella 2025]`. Set a `Max Spend Cap` of $500 per hour to prevent runaway costs. Finally, enable `Audience Layering` to exclude users who have already converted (e.g., clicked a link in the last 24 hours). Now, when your post hits a PVS of 91 during the headliner’s encore, the system will autonomously push $150 into a high-bid ad set before you even check your phone.

## Industry Best Practices

### 1. Use the “Anti-Peak” Scheduling Window
Most brands blast content during headliner sets (9 PM–11 PM), creating a noise floor. Instead, schedule your highest-PVS content for the “golden hour gap” (5:30 PM–6:30 PM) and the post-headliner lull (11:30 PM–12:30 AM). Our data shows a 40% higher organic engagement rate during these windows because the algorithm competition is lower, and users are actively scrolling while waiting for the next act or leaving the grounds. Set your `Scheduling Offset` to `+90 minutes` from the official set time to catch the wave of post-performance UGC.

### 2. Never Boost a Post Without a “Sonic Signature” Match
If your content doesn’t contain an audio fingerprint that matches a currently playing or recently played set, do not allocate ad spend to it. The PVS model is heavily weighted on audio-visual congruence. A static photo of a stage will always score below a 10-second clip with the actual live audio. Force your team to use the `Audio Sync` feature on every asset. If the audio is not from the festival, the system should flag it with a red `Audio Anomaly` warning. This single rule has reduced wasted ad spend by 60% in beta tests with mid-tier artists.
