## Overview

**coachellaviral** is a high-performance SaaS engine engineered to dominate Coachella-related search intent and social amplification. It combines real-time festival trend scraping, AI-driven content mutation, and automated multi-platform syndication to turn music festival buzz into indexed, shareable, and monetizable viral assets. This is not a generic SEO tool — it’s a tactical system built for the chaos of festival season.

## Core Features

### 1. Real-Time Festival Trend Injection Engine (FTIE)
coachellaviral continuously scrapes Coachella-specific social signals (Reddit threads, TikTok hashtag velocity, Setlist.fm updates, Instagram Stories geotags) and injects them into your content pipeline. The engine identifies breakout moments — like a surprise guest or a stage malfunction — within 90 seconds and auto-generates SEO-optimized micro-content (headlines, alt text, meta descriptions) targeting those exact signals.

### 2. Multi-Format Viral Mutation & Syndication Pipeline
One input, 12+ outputs. The system takes a single seed piece (e.g., a blog post or video transcript) and mutates it into platform-native formats: TikTok scripts with timing cues, YouTube Shorts captions with hashtag clusters, Twitter threads with reply bait, Pinterest pins with alt-text SEO, and Reddit-style AMA prompts. Each mutation is optimized for both human engagement and search crawlers, with automatic schema markup injection (Article, VideoObject, Event).

### 3. Festival-Specific Content Decay & Refresh Automation
Most festival content dies 48 hours after the event. coachellaviral uses a decay-aware scheduler to automatically repurpose, refresh, and re-publish your content across multiple cycles: pre-festival hype, live-day rapid updates, post-festival recap, and “throwback” revival 6 months later. Each cycle adjusts keyword targets (e.g., “Coachella 2025 lineup” → “Coachella 2025 best moments” → “Coachella 2025 vs 2026”) and updates internal linking structures to maintain topical authority.

## How to Use

### Step 1: Configure the Trend Source & Content Seed
Start by defining your content seed — a raw video transcript, a setlist, or a blog draft. Then, in the dashboard, select your trend sources: Reddit (r/Coachella), TikTok API (hashtag velocity), and Google Trends (keyword spike detection). Set your target language (en) and geographic focus (US). The engine will begin scanning for real-time signals and tag your seed with the most relevant emerging keywords (e.g., “Doja Cat surprise set”, “Yuma tent audio issues”).

### Step 2: Run the Viral Mutation Generator
Navigate to the **Mutation Engine** tab. Select your output platforms: TikTok, YouTube Shorts, Twitter/X, Pinterest, and Reddit. For each platform, set the tone (e.g., “hype”, “critical review”, “behind-the-scenes”). Click **Generate Mutations**. The system will produce platform-specific drafts, each with embedded SEO metadata (title tags, alt text, hashtag clusters, canonical URLs). Review the mutation logs — each entry shows the original source snippet and the mutated output with a confidence score (based on trend alignment).

### Step 3: Deploy the Decay-Aware Publishing Schedule
Go to the **Content Calendar** and click **Auto-Schedule**. The engine will calculate optimal publish timestamps based on festival phases: pre-festival (T-14 days), live-day (every 2 hours during peak activity), post-festival (T+1 to T+7 days), and revival (T+180 days). Each scheduled post includes automatic internal linking to related mutations (e.g., a TikTok script links to a blog recap) and a fallback redirect plan in case of broken links. Confirm the schedule and hit **Deploy**. The system will handle publishing via API integrations (WordPress, Shopify, YouTube, etc.).

## Best Practices

### 1. Use the “Trend Velocity” Filter to Avoid Saturation
Don’t chase every spike. Set a minimum velocity threshold (e.g., 500 mentions/hour on TikTok) to filter out noise. coachellaviral’s dashboard shows a real-time heatmap of keyword saturation. Targeting a niche micro-trend (e.g., “Coachella 2025 vegan food vendors”) often yields higher conversion rates than competing for “Coachella 2025” head terms. The engine’s **Trend Clustering** feature groups related low-competition keywords into a single content cluster — use this for long-tail dominance.

### 2. Enable Cross-Platform Canonical Linking for Content Clusters
When running mutations, always enable **Canonical Cluster Mode**. This creates a master URL (typically your blog post) and sets all mutated content (TikToks, tweets, pins) as syndicated copies with `rel=canonical` pointing back to the master. This prevents duplicate content penalties while still allowing platform-specific formatting. For example, a YouTube Shorts description can include a canonical link to the full video on your site, preserving SEO equity while driving traffic.
