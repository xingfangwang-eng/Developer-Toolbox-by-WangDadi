# coachellaviral - COA_016

## Overview

`coachellaviral` is a specialized SaaS toolkit designed to amplify organic reach and social engagement around major music festivals, with a primary focus on Coachella. It combines real-time trend detection, multi-platform content syndication, and audience sentiment mapping to help marketers, influencers, and event promoters dominate the festival conversation.

## Core Features

### 1. Real-Time Viral Signal Detection Engine
`coachellaviral` ingests live data from Twitter/X, TikTok, Reddit, and Instagram to identify emerging viral spikes before they peak. Using NLP-driven topic clustering and propagation velocity scoring, it flags content themes, hashtags, and audio clips that are trending within a 15-minute window. You get push notifications and a ranked feed of "pre-viral" signals tailored to your brand.

### 2. Multi-Platform Content Orchestrator
Automatically repurpose and schedule festival-related content across TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts, and X (Twitter). The orchestrator adapts video aspect ratios, caption lengths, and posting cadence based on each platform's algorithm preferences. It also supports A/B testing of hooks and CTAs across platforms, with real-time performance dashboards.

### 3. Audience Sentiment & Geo-Heat Mapping
Track audience mood and location-based engagement in real time. The tool overlays sentiment data on a heat map of the festival grounds (or surrounding areas), showing where positive, negative, or neutral conversations are clustering. Use this to optimize booth placement, influencer meetups, or pop-up content strategies.

## How to Use

### 1. Configure Your Viral Signal Dashboard
- **Step**: Navigate to the **Signals** tab and connect your social media accounts (Twitter/X, TikTok, Instagram, Reddit).  
- **Action**: Set up keyword filters for your brand, artist, or festival edition (e.g., "Coachella 2025", "Desert Trip", "Sahara Tent").  
- **Optimization**: Adjust the **Velocity Threshold** slider to filter out noise. A threshold of 0.75 or higher ensures you only see signals with rapid engagement growth.  
- **Output**: You'll receive a live feed of pre-viral content candidates with engagement velocity scores and suggested posting times.

### 2. Launch a Multi-Platform Content Campaign
- **Step**: Go to the **Orchestrator** section and upload your base video or image asset.  
- **Action**: Select target platforms (TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts, X). The tool auto-generates platform-optimized versions: 9:16 for TikTok/Reels, 16:9 for YouTube, and 1:1 for X.  
- **Optimization**: Use the **Hook Tester** to run A/B tests on your first 3 seconds of video. The system will push the winning variant to all platforms after 30 minutes of data.  
- **Output**: A campaign dashboard showing per-platform impressions, completion rates, and share velocity.

### 3. Deploy Geo-Heat Mapping for On-Ground Tactics
- **Step**: Open the **Heat Map** module and set your event location (e.g., Empire Polo Club, Indio, CA).  
- **Action**: Select a time range (past 24 hours or live) and filter by sentiment type (positive, negative, neutral).  
- **Optimization**: Overlay your own geo-tagged content (booth locations, influencer check-ins) to see correlation between physical presence and online sentiment spikes.  
- **Output**: A downloadable heat map CSV and a real-time map view. Use this to decide where to deploy pop-up activations or adjust influencer schedules.

## Best Practices

### 1. Pre-Festival Signal Harvesting
Start monitoring viral signals 3–4 weeks before the festival. Use `coachellaviral` to identify early-stage trends (e.g., "Coachella outfits 2025", "camping hacks") and create content that rides the wave before it peaks. Most brands jump in during the festival weekend—being early gives you a compounding advantage.

### 2. Cross-Platform Sentiment Alignment
Don't treat sentiment data as a single metric. Cross-reference positive sentiment on TikTok with neutral/negative sentiment on X (Twitter) to identify friction points. For example, if a lineup announcement gets high engagement on TikTok but low sentiment on X, your content strategy should lean into TikTok's energy while addressing X's concerns (e.g., "We hear you—here's why this year's lineup is actually stacked").
