The engine scrapes and parses Coachella’s official lineup, artist social feeds, and Reddit/r/Coachella sentiment in real time. It then auto-generates a content calendar optimized for each artist’s announcement, set time, and after-party buzz. You don’t schedule posts—you schedule *reactions*.

## How to Use

### Step 1: Inject the Lineup into the Engine
On the dashboard, paste the official Coachella 2025 lineup URL or upload a CSV. The engine will fingerprint each artist by genre, follower count, and past Coachella performance metrics. It then assigns a “viral probability score” (0–100) per artist. Use this to prioritize which acts to build campaigns around.

### Step 2: Generate a Pool of 100+ Pre-Optimized Assets
Click “Generate Campaign” and select your target languages. The engine will produce:
- 30 short-form video scripts (15–60s, optimized for TikTok/Reels)
- 40 text posts with auto-generated hashtag clusters (e.g., #CoachellaViral, #DesertDaze, #IndioHeat)
- 30 visual caption variants with emoji density and sentiment calibration

Each asset includes a predicted engagement lift score. Export as CSV or push directly to Buffer/Hootsuite via API.

### Step 3: Deploy with Geo-Temporal Triggers
Set up geo-fenced automation: when the engine detects a post from a specific stage (e.g., Sahara Tent) exceeding a viral threshold (e.g., 10k shares in 30 minutes), it auto-publishes your pre-approved content into the same geo-zone. This creates a “piggyback” effect—your content rides the wave of organic virality without looking like spam.

## Best Practices

### 1. Use the “Cold Start” Window
Coachella’s first lineup announcement day (usually mid-January) is the highest-traffic, lowest-competition window. Run your campaign generator 48 hours before the official drop. Pre-schedule 10–15 reaction posts that reference leaked rumors or past lineups. This builds domain authority with the Coachella community before the main event.

### 2. Layer Artist-Specific Hashtags with Geo-Hashtags
Don’t just use #Coachella. Pair artist hashtags (e.g., #BadBunnyCoachella) with location-specific tags (e.g., #PalmSpringsVibes, #IndioNights). The engine’s multilingual mode will auto-generate these in the target language. For example, a Japanese-language post about BAD BUNNY should include #バッドバニー and #インディオ砂漠. This increases discoverability across both global and local feeds.
