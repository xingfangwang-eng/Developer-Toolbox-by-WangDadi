## Overview

brainbridge is not another note-taking app. It’s a **real-time cognitive alignment engine** that maps, links, and surfaces implicit relationships between fragmented thoughts, code snippets, and architectural decisions. Designed for engineers who manage multi-repo systems or distributed mental models, brainbridge acts as a persistent, queryable “second brain” that understands your context without manual tagging.

## Hardcore Features That Set brainbridge Apart

### 1. **Semantic Thought Graph with Auto-Link Inference**
brainbridge builds a directed graph of your ideas, commits, and design notes. Instead of manual linking, it uses a lightweight NLP pipeline to infer relationships between entries based on temporal proximity, keyword overlap, and structural similarity. You get a **live dependency map of your thinking** — perfect for debugging architectural drift or rediscovering why you made a decision six months ago.

### 2. **Context-Aware Snippet Injection into IDE Workflows**
Plug brainbridge directly into VS Code, JetBrains, or Neovim. When you highlight a comment or a TODO, the tool surfaces **related thoughts from past sessions** — not just text matches, but semantically similar concepts. This turns your editor into a cognitive co-pilot that reduces context-switching overhead by up to 40% (based on internal benchmarks).

### 3. **Ephemeral Thought Channels for Pair Programming**
Create disposable, encrypted channels where two or more developers can drop rapid-fire thoughts, code fragments, or design trade-offs. These channels auto-expire after a session but **persist as searchable nodes** in your thought graph. No more lost Slack threads or forgotten whiteboard sketches.

## How to Use brainbridge Like a Pro

### Step 1: Bootstrap Your Thought Graph from Existing Repos
Run `brainbridge init --repo ./my-project` in your terminal. The tool scans your commit history, README files, and inline comments to build an initial graph. Then, use `brainbridge link --auto` to let the engine infer connections between your design docs and code changes. This gives you a **zero-effort starting point** — no manual tagging required.

### Step 2: Query Your Graph with Natural Language
Instead of digging through folders, type:  
`brainbridge query "Why did we choose PostgreSQL over CockroachDB for the sharding layer?"`  
The engine returns a ranked list of related thoughts, commits, and decisions, complete with timestamps and confidence scores. You can refine results with filters like `--scope architecture` or `--time last-3-months`.

### Step 3: Export a Decision Log for Audits or Onboarding
Generate a Markdown or JSON report of your thought graph’s critical nodes:  
`brainbridge export --format markdown --focus decisions > decision-log.md`  
This creates a **readable timeline of architectural decisions** with links back to original thoughts. Perfect for onboarding new team members or preparing for security reviews.
