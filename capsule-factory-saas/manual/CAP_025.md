**A production-grade, multi-tenant SaaS platform for real-time capsule manufacturing orchestration, quality traceability, and yield optimization.**

## What Is capsule-factory-saas?

capsule-factory-saas is a cloud-native, API-first tool purpose-built for pharmaceutical and nutraceutical capsule production lines. It eliminates spreadsheet chaos, bridges the gap between PLCs and ERP systems, and provides deterministic batch control for hard/soft gelatin and vegetarian capsule runs.

Unlike generic MES platforms, capsule-factory-saas is designed to handle the unique constraints of capsule manufacturing — from fill-weight variance and shell moisture drift to encapsulation speed tuning and dissolution testing triggers.

---

## Core Features

### 1. Real-Time Fill-Weight Tuning with Closed-Loop Feedback
Integrate directly with on-line checkweighers and capsule fillers (e.g., MG2, Bosch, IMA). The system automatically adjusts fill parameters (dosator depth, tamping force, vacuum level) every 10 seconds based on live weight distribution. Out-of-tolerance capsules are flagged and diverted without halting the line — reducing waste by up to 18% in high-speed production.

### 2. Batch Genealogy with Multi-Node Traceability
Every capsule batch is linked to its raw material lot (gelatin, HPMC, active powder), encapsulation machine ID, operator shift, temperature/humidity log, and dissolution test results. The system generates a cryptographically signed “batch fingerprint” that can be exported as a JSON-LD document for FDA 21 CFR Part 11 audits or EU Annex 11 compliance.

### 3. Predictive Shell Moisture Management
Using historical sensor data and ambient conditions, capsule-factory-saas predicts moisture regain in capsule shells before they enter the encapsulation hopper. It sends proactive alerts to adjust dehumidifier setpoints or pause feeding when shell moisture exceeds 12% RH — preventing sticking, splitting, or weight drift downstream.

## How to Use

### 1. Connect Your Encapsulation Line via OPC UA or MQTT
First, configure the data bridge between capsule-factory-saas and your encapsulation line’s PLC. Navigate to **Settings > Device Integration** and select your preferred protocol (OPC UA, MQTT, or Modbus TCP). Enter the endpoint URI and authentication credentials. The system will auto-discover available tags (e.g., fill weight, tamping force, capsule speed). Map at least three mandatory tags: `fill_weight_avg`, `capsule_speed`, and `reject_count`. Once connected, the dashboard will start streaming live metrics within 15 seconds.

### 2. Set Up a Batch Recipe with Dynamic Tolerances
Go to **Production > Recipes** and click “Create New Batch Recipe.” Define the target fill weight (e.g., 500 mg ± 3%), shell type (hard gelatin, size 00), and encapsulation speed (e.g., 100,000 capsules/hour). Under “Advanced Tuning,” enable **Closed-Loop Fill Control** and set the correction interval to 10 seconds. Optionally, attach a dissolution specification file (PDF or CSV). Save the recipe and assign it to a specific encapsulation machine.

### 3. Monitor and Trigger Automated Adjustments
Open the **Live Batch Dashboard**. The main gauge shows current fill weight distribution with a moving average line. If the system detects a drift beyond your tolerance window, it will automatically trigger a correction command to the filler. You’ll see a log entry with the timestamp, old vs. new parameter values, and the operator who acknowledged the adjustment. Use the **Export Batch Report** button to generate a PDF summary containing all sensor logs, adjustments, and quality checks — ready for your QA team.

## Best Practices

### 1. Calibrate Your Checkweigher Weekly and Sync the Offset
Even with closed-loop tuning, physical sensor drift occurs. Schedule a weekly calibration routine where you run a known-weight reference set (e.g., 10 capsules at 500 mg). Enter the measured offset in capsule-factory-saas under **Settings > Calibration Offsets**. This prevents the system from over-correcting based on a drifting sensor, maintaining stable production over long runs.

### 2. Use Batch Fingerprints for Supplier Quality Audits
When you receive a raw material lot (e.g., HPMC shells from a new supplier), run a small test batch and generate its batch fingerprint. Compare the fingerprint’s moisture sensitivity index and fill consistency score against your historical baseline. If the deviation exceeds 5%, reject the lot before it enters full production. This practice reduces the risk of a costly line shutdown due to incompatible shell behavior.
