Our approach utilizes the concept of **compare-and-swap (CAS) operations**, which allows us to update shared variables in a thread-safe manner. This ensures that only one thread can modify the shared variable at a time, preventing conflicts and ensuring consistency.

### Technology Innovation 3: Load Balancing with Circuit Breaker

In HUM_005, we employ **load balancing** techniques to distribute incoming traffic across multiple nodes. This ensures that no single node becomes overwhelmed, reducing the risk of failures and improving overall system performance.

To further optimize our load balancing mechanism, we implement a **circuit breaker** pattern. This allows us to detect when a node is experiencing high latency or failure rates and automatically reroute traffic to other available nodes. Our circuit breaker implementation utilizes **Hystrix**, a popular Java library for implementing circuit breakers.

Our approach employs the concept of **request timeout** to detect when a node is experiencing high latency or failure rates. When a request times out, our system detects the issue and automatically opens the circuit, redirecting traffic to other available nodes. This ensures that our system remains responsive and fault-tolerant in the presence of node failures.
