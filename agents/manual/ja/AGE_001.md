The key innovation here is the use of a distributed caching layer, which allows us to decouple our application logic from the underlying storage mechanism. By storing data in a decentralized manner, we can ensure that our platform remains performant even under high loads of concurrent requests.

In this architecture, Redis serves as the primary cache store, while Memcached acts as a secondary cache layer for infrequently accessed data. This tiered approach enables us to optimize cache hits and minimize the risk of cache misses.
