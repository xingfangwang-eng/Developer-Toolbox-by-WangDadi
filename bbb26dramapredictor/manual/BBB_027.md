## What is bbb26dramapredictor?

bbb26dramapredictor is a **real-time NLP + social sentiment analysis engine** designed to predict drama escalation in the Brazilian reality show **BBB26** (Big Brother Brasil). It ingests live Twitter/X streams, Reddit threads, and Globo transcripts, then outputs **tension scores, conflict probability vectors, and participant interaction heatmaps** — all within a sub-10-second latency window.

Unlike generic sentiment tools, bbb26dramapredictor is **tuned for reality TV discourse**: it understands sarcasm, fan rivalries, and meme-driven amplification loops.

## Core Features

### 1. Multi-Modal Drama Vectorizer
Ingests **text, emoji density, and reaction GIF metadata** from live social feeds. Uses a fine-tuned BERT model (trained on 3 seasons of BBB transcripts) to classify each post into one of 12 drama archetypes (e.g., *Strategic Betrayal*, *Emotional Meltdown*, *Alliance Fracture*). Outputs a **drama vector** per participant every 30 seconds.

### 2. Tension Heatmap API
Exposes a RESTful endpoint (`/v1/heatmap`) that returns a **time-series matrix** of interaction tension between any two housemates. Each cell contains a normalized score (0.0–1.0) derived from co-mention frequency, sentiment polarity, and reply-chain depth. Ideal for building dashboards or live overlays for stream overlays.

### 3. Predictive Flashpoint Alerts
A **probabilistic model** (LightGBM + temporal attention) trained on historical BBB26 data. It emits alerts when the system detects a **>70% probability** of a high-drama event within the next 15 minutes. Alerts include the predicted participants, likely trigger topic, and confidence interval. Webhook-ready for Discord, Slack, or custom notification pipelines.

## Best Practices

### 1. Calibrate the Drama Threshold Per Show Phase
During the first week of BBB26, drama signals are often false positives (friendly banter). **Set your confidence threshold to 0.85** using the `--confidence` flag. After Week 3 (alliance dynamics solidify), lower it to 0.65 to catch subtle fractures. Use the `bbb26dramapredictor --auto-calibrate` flag to let the model learn from weekly voting patterns.

### 2. Combine with Official Polling Data for Higher Precision
The model’s accuracy jumps from 72% to 89% when you **merge its output with Globo’s public voting percentages**. Use the `/v1/merge` endpoint to fuse drama vectors with poll data. This creates a **dual-signal trigger** — high drama + low voting gap = near-certain blowup. Implement a sliding window of 3 hours to avoid stale poll data.
