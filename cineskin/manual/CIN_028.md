# cineskin - CIN_028

## Overview

cineskin is a next-gen SaaS tool engineered for filmmakers, VFX artists, and post-production teams who demand precision-grade skin grading and digital makeup directly inside their video editing pipeline. Designed for performance and color fidelity, cineskin eliminates the guesswork from skin tone matching, texture smoothing, and cinematic stylization.

## What Makes cineskin Different?

Unlike generic color grading plugins, cineskin is purpose-built for **human skin detection and manipulation** across diverse lighting conditions and ethnicities. It's not just a filter—it's a post-production skin engine.

## Core Features

### 1. Spectral Skin Detection Engine
Leverages a proprietary ML model trained on over 200,000 annotated frames from film and broadcast datasets. It isolates skin regions with sub-pixel accuracy, distinguishing between skin, hair, and background textures even in low-light or high-contrast scenes. No more unwanted edge artifacts or blown-out highlights.

### 2. Non-Destructive Texture Layering
Apply digital makeup, de-aging, or stylized skin looks without flattening the original texture. cineskin uses a multi-pass compositing system that preserves pore detail, subsurface scattering, and micro-shadows. Adjust opacity, blending mode, and region masks per layer—all in real-time.

### 3. LUT-Aware Color Space Bridge
Seamlessly integrates with your existing ACES, Rec.709, or custom LUT pipeline. cineskin automatically detects your project's color space and applies transformations without clipping or banding. Export skin-only LUTs for consistent looks across multiple shots.

## How to Use

### 1. Calibrate Skin Detection for Your Shot
Load your clip into the editor and open the cineskin panel. Click **"Auto-Detect"** to run the initial skin mask. If the mask misses areas (e.g., under harsh shadows or specular highlights), manually paint with the **"Add/Subtract Skin Region"** brush. Use the **"Edge Feather"** slider to soften mask boundaries for natural blending.

### 2. Apply a Cinematic Skin Grade
From the **"Looks Library"**, choose a preset like **"Golden Hour"** or **"Cool Tonal"**. Adjust the **"Global Intensity"** to control how much the grade affects skin. For fine control, open the **"HSL Skin Curve"**—this modifies only the selected skin region's hue, saturation, and luminance, leaving the background untouched.

### 3. Export a Reusable Skin LUT
Once your grade is set, click **"Export Skin LUT"**. Choose a 33x33 .cube format and save it to your project folder. In your NLE, apply this LUT to other shots with similar lighting. For batch processing, use the **"Match to Reference"** tool to align multiple clips to the same skin tone profile.

## Best Practices

### 1. Always Calibrate Per Scene
Don't rely on a single skin detection profile for an entire timeline. Lighting changes between shots—especially in outdoor vs. indoor scenes. Run the auto-detection and manual touch-up for each new location to avoid inconsistent masks.

### 2. Use Skin LUTs for Multi-Camera Edits
When editing interviews or multi-cam footage, apply your final skin grade as a LUT to all angles. This ensures talent's skin tone remains consistent across cuts, regardless of camera brand or lens color cast. Save time by reusing the same LUT in DaVinci Resolve or Premiere Pro.
