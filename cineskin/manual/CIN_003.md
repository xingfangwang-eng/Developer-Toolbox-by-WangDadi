# cineskin - CIN_003

## Overview

cineskin is a next-generation film-grade skin design and video effects tool built for post-production pros who demand pixel-perfect control over digital skin textures, lighting simulation, and real-time compositing. It bridges the gap between VFX pipelines and editorial workflows, enabling seamless integration with industry-standard NLEs and compositing software.

## What is cineskin?

cineskin is a specialized video effects engine that applies hyper-realistic skin textures, dynamic lighting overlays, and procedural aging effects to footage—without degrading resolution or introducing artifacts. It’s designed for colorists, VFX artists, and motion designers working on high-end film, TV, and commercial projects.

## Core Features

### 1. **Real-Time Skin Texture Layering with Subsurface Scattering**
cineskin’s proprietary texture engine allows you to stack multiple skin layers (pores, wrinkles, blemishes, moisture) with adjustable subsurface scattering. Each layer can be keyframed and masked independently, giving you granular control over how light interacts with digital skin in motion.

### 2. **AI-Powered Lighting Simulation for Consistent Skin Tone Across Shots**
The tool includes an AI-driven lighting analyzer that matches skin tone and texture to the scene’s ambient light. It automatically adjusts highlights, shadows, and color temperature to maintain continuity across cuts—saving hours of manual grading.

### 3. **Procedural Aging & De-Aging with Temporal Coherence**
cineskin applies non-destructive aging or de-aging effects that follow facial anatomy and motion. The temporal coherence engine ensures smooth transitions between frames, eliminating flicker and jitter common in traditional frame-by-frame effects.

## How to Use

### Step 1: **Import and Analyze Footage**
Drag your clip onto the cineskin timeline. The AI engine automatically scans the first 30 frames to detect skin regions, lighting conditions, and motion vectors. A heatmap overlay appears, highlighting areas where texture layers will be applied. Fine-tune detection sensitivity in the **Analyzer Panel** if needed.

### Step 2: **Layer Skin Textures with Masking**
Open the **Texture Stack** panel. Click **+Add Layer** and choose from presets (e.g., “Fine Pores”, “Moisture Gloss”, “Weathering”). Use the built-in rotoscoping brush to mask specific areas—like the forehead or cheeks—for targeted effects. Adjust opacity, blend mode, and subsurface scattering sliders in real time while scrubbing the timeline.

### Step 3: **Apply Lighting Simulation and Export**
Switch to the **Lighting Sim** tab. Click **Auto-Match** to let cineskin sync skin lighting with the scene’s key light, fill light, and rim light. Preview the result in split-screen mode. Once satisfied, export with either **ProRes 4444** (for editorial) or **OpenEXR** (for VFX compositing). The tool generates a sidecar LUT for easy reapplication.

## Best Practices

### 1. **Use Reference Frames for Consistent Lighting**
Always capture a reference frame with a gray card or color checker in the same lighting environment. cineskin’s lighting simulation works best when it has a baseline to calibrate against. This reduces manual tweaks across multiple shots.

### 2. **Layer Textures from Subtle to Extreme**
Start with low-opacity base layers (e.g., skin smoothing or pore reduction) and build up to high-opacity effects like sweat or scars. This prevents over-processing and preserves natural skin movement, especially in close-ups where every detail matters.
