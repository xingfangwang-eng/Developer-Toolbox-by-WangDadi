# cineskin - CIN_011

## Overview

cineskin is a high-performance, metadata-driven movie skin design and video effects tool built for post-production pipelines that demand precision. It eliminates the friction between creative intent and technical execution, enabling VFX artists and colorists to define, iterate, and deploy cinematic looks without leaving their editing environment.

## Core Features

### 1. Frame-Aware LUT Injection with Temporal Noise Gating
Unlike static LUTs, cineskin analyzes each frame’s luminance histogram and motion vectors in real time. It dynamically applies color grading curves that adapt to scene cuts, lighting changes, and camera movement. The temporal noise gate prevents color shift artifacts during fast transitions, ensuring every frame stays consistent without manual keyframing.

### 2. Non-Destructive Skin Tone Isolation via AI Semantic Segmentation
cineskin uses a lightweight ONNX model to isolate skin regions at the sub-pixel level, separating them from hair, clothing, and backgrounds. You can apply skin-specific textures (pores, subsurface scattering, glow) without affecting the rest of the frame. The model runs locally on GPU, with inference times under 5ms per 4K frame.

### 3. Multi-Layer Texture Stack with Real-Time GPU Compositing
Stack up to 8 texture layers (film grain, lens flare, chromatic aberration, dust, scratches) and blend them using custom alpha masks or luminance-based masks. Each layer has its own transform, opacity, and blend mode. The compositor runs on Vulkan compute shaders, delivering 60fps playback even with all layers active on a 1080p timeline.

## How to Use

### 1. Integrate cineskin into Your DaVinci Resolve Pipeline
Open Resolve, go to `Workspace > Scripts > cineskin`, and click “Activate OFX plugin.” The plugin registers itself as a node in the Color page. Drag it onto any clip, then in the inspector, load a `.csk` preset file. The plugin will automatically parse the metadata (white balance, contrast curve, grain intensity) and apply it to the timeline in real time.

### 2. Build a Custom Skin Texture Layer
In the cineskin panel, enable “AI Skin Mask.” Click “Train on Frame” and select a reference frame where the actor’s face is clearly visible. The model will generate a semantic mask. Then, under the “Texture Stack” tab, add a new layer, choose “Skin Pores” from the texture library, and set blend mode to “Overlay.” Adjust the mask feather to 2px and opacity to 40%. The texture will only apply to skin regions, leaving eyes and lips untouched.

### 3. Export a Reusable Preset for Your Team
After finishing your grade, click the “Export Preset” button in the top toolbar. Choose “Include All Layers” and “Embed LUT.” Save the `.csk` file to your shared network drive. Your teammates can import it via the “Load Preset” button, and cineskin will automatically remap the LUT to match their clip’s color space (Rec.709, Rec.2020, or ACES). No manual re-grading needed.

## Best Practices

- **Use Reference Frames for AI Mask Calibration**  
  Always train the semantic segmentation model on a frame with good skin exposure and no motion blur. If the actor turns their head rapidly, the mask may flicker. To fix this, enable “Temporal Stabilization” in the AI Skin Mask settings, which averages the mask over the last 3 frames. This reduces flicker without sacrificing edge sharpness.

- **Keep Texture Stack Layers Under 6 for Real-Time Playback**  
  While cineskin supports 8 texture layers, stacking more than 6 on a 4K timeline may drop below 24fps on mid-range GPUs (RTX 3060 or equivalent). If you need heavy texturing, pre-render a composite layer in cineskin’s “Bake to Clip” mode, then apply the baked clip back to the timeline. This frees GPU resources and maintains smooth scrubbing.
