# cineskin - CIN_029

## Overview

cineskin is a next-gen film-grade skin retouching and color grading engine designed for post-production pipelines. Unlike generic video filters, cineskin leverages per-frame temporal coherence and AI-driven skin segmentation to preserve texture while applying cinematic color palettes. It’s built for editors, colorists, and VFX artists who demand frame-accurate control without sacrificing performance.

## Core Features

### 1. Temporal Skin Lock with Motion-Aware Masking
cineskin’s proprietary **Temporal Skin Lock** algorithm tracks skin regions across frames using optical flow + deep segmentation. It eliminates flickering and “skin bleed” even during rapid head turns or occlusions. You can define custom skin zones (e.g., face vs. hands) and apply independent grades—no manual rotoscoping required.

### 2. LUT-Aware Dynamic Skin Grading
Not just a 3D LUT overlay—cineskin analyzes the luminance and chrominance of your reference LUT and adapts skin tones accordingly. It prevents unnatural skin shifts (e.g., turning faces green or magenta) while preserving the intended mood. Supports ACES, Rec.709, and custom LUTs.

### 3. Real-Time GPU-Accelerated Preview with Proxy Fallback
Render 4K timelines in real time on mid-range GPUs. cineskin automatically generates proxy clips for complex scenes, switching to full-res preview on demand. The engine uses Vulkan/Metal backends and supports multi-GPU setups for heavy comps.

## How to Use

### Step 1: Import & Auto-Detect Skin Regions
Drag your footage into the cineskin node graph. Click **“Analyze”** to run the initial segmentation pass. The tool highlights detected skin zones in a heatmap overlay. Adjust the **“Skin Sensitivity”** slider (0–100) to fine-tune detection for different skin tones, lighting conditions, or prosthetic makeup. Use the **“Mask Brush”** to paint corrections directly on the timeline.

### Step 2: Apply a Cinematic Grade with Skin Protection
Load a reference LUT via the **“Grade Panel”** (drag & drop .cube files). Enable **“Skin Protection Mode”** from the dropdown. cineskin will compute a per-pixel blend between the LUT’s global transformation and a neutral skin curve. Tweak **“Protection Strength”** (0–1) to control how much skin deviates from the LUT. Preview split-screen with original vs. graded.

### Step 3: Export with Metadata & Burn-In Options
Go to **Export** tab. Choose output format (ProRes, DNxHR, H.264/H.265). Check **“Embed Skin Mask”** to save the alpha channel as a separate matte for further compositing. Enable **“Burn-in Skin Overlay”** for client reviews—adds a subtle outline around skin regions. Click **“Render Queue”** to batch process.

## Best Practices

### Practice 1: Use Skin Protection as a Layer, Not a Filter
Don’t rely on a single pass. Chain multiple cineskin nodes: one for global grade (with low protection), one for skin-only adjustments (high protection). This gives you granular control over highlights, shadows, and saturation without muddying skin tones.

### Practice 2: Calibrate Skin Sensitivity Per Shot
Always run the **“Sensitivity Calibration”** tool on the first frame of a new scene. If you’re grading an interview with mixed lighting, lock the mask to the subject’s face using **“Anchor Frame”**—cineskin will propagate that mask across the clip, reducing false positives (e.g., background wood textures).
