# cineskin - CIN_004

## Overview

cineskin is a next‑generation SaaS tool for cinematic skin retouching, color grading, and real‑time video effect layering — purpose‑built for post‑production pros who demand pixel‑perfect consistency across every frame.

## What is cineskin?

cineskin is a cloud‑native video effects engine that automates skin‑tone‑aware color grading, dynamic texture mapping, and frame‑accurate effect sequencing. It eliminates manual rotoscoping and guesswork, letting you focus on creative direction instead of tedious corrections.

## Core Features

### 1. **Skin‑Aware LUT Chain**  
cineskin’s proprietary ML model isolates facial skin regions from hair, makeup, and background in real time. It applies a separate LUT chain to skin vs. non‑skin areas — preserving natural undertones while allowing aggressive grade on the rest. No more blown‑out faces or muddy complexions.

### 2. **Frame‑Consistent Effect Propagation**  
Define a skin texture or color effect on a single keyframe, and cineskin auto‑propagates it across the entire shot using optical flow and temporal coherence. Handles occlusions, head turns, and lighting changes without jitter or flicker.

### 3. **Multi‑Layer Skin Compositing**  
Stack up to 8 independent skin‑effect layers (e.g., foundation, highlight, contour, grain) with per‑layer opacity and blend modes. Each layer can be masked by skin zone (forehead, cheeks, chin) — think “Photoshop for video skin.”

## How to Use

### 1. **Import & Define Skin Zones**  
Upload your footage (MP4, MOV, or ProRes). cineskin automatically detects all faces and generates a skin‑zone mask. Fine‑tune using the zone editor: adjust eyebrow, lip, and hair exclusion regions. Press `CMD+Shift+M` to toggle mask overlay.

### 2. **Apply & Chain LUTs**  
From the LUT panel, drag a base grade onto the “Non‑Skin” channel. Then, under “Skin Channel,” select a custom LUT or use the built‑in “Cinema Warm” preset. Adjust intensity sliders (0–100%) per channel. Use the “Blend” slider to cross‑fade between the two LUTs for natural transitions.

### 3. **Propagate & Export**  
Set your effect keyframe at frame 00:00:10. Click **Propagate → Temporal Coherence**. cineskin processes the entire clip and shows a diff overlay. Review, adjust propagation strength (default 0.8), then export as a flattened ProRes 4444 or an XML/AAF for your NLE. Use `CMD+E` for quick export.

## Best Practices

- **Always calibrate skin‑zone masks on the first frame of a new scene.** Lighting changes between scenes cause the ML model to shift its detection — a quick zone adjustment prevents color bleed into hair or background.  
- **Use the “Diff Overlay” mode before final export.** This highlights every pixel that changed between frames. If you see flicker in the diff layer, dial down the propagation strength or add a temporal smoothing keyframe at the transition point.
