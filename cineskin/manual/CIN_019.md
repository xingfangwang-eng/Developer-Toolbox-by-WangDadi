# cineskin - CIN_019

## Overview

cineskin is a precision-engineered SaaS tool for cinematic skin design, real-time video effects, and post-production enhancement. Built for motion designers, VFX artists, and indie filmmakers, it streamlines the pipeline from raw footage to polished output with zero frame loss and full LUT compatibility.

## Core Features

### 1. **Dynamic Skin Mapping Engine**
Apply hyper-realistic skin textures and color grading directly onto moving subjects. The engine tracks facial landmarks and body contours in real time, enabling seamless integration of custom skin overlays, aging effects, or stylized dermatextures without manual rotoscoping.

### 2. **Frame-Accurate Effect Sequencing**
Chain video effects with sub-frame precision using a node-based timeline. Each effect node supports keyframe interpolation, blending modes, and mask constraints. Perfect for multi-layer compositing where timing is critical—no render lag, no dropped frames.

### 3. **AI-Assisted LUT Harmonizer**
Automatically match the color profile of your skin effects to the ambient scene lighting. The AI analyzes histogram data, white balance, and dominant hues, then generates a corrective LUT that preserves skin tone integrity while blending into any cinematic grade.

## How to Use

### Step 1: Import and Map Source Media
Drag your footage into the workspace. cineskin auto-detects scene cuts and extracts a frame sequence. Use the **Subject Lock** tool to define the skin region: draw a rough polygon around the face or body, and the engine will refine boundaries using edge detection and temporal smoothing.

### Step 2: Configure Skin Effect Parameters
Open the **Skin Shader** panel. Adjust **Diffuse Intensity** (0–1.0) to control texture visibility, **Specular Rolloff** for realistic light reflection, and **Subsurface Scattering Radius** (recommended 2–8 mm for human skin). Preview changes in real time with split-screen comparison.

### Step 3: Sequence and Export
Switch to the **Effect Sequencer**. Drag your skin effect node onto the timeline. Set keyframes for opacity, mask position, or color shift. Use the **Render Queue** to batch export with your chosen codec (ProRes 422 or H.265 recommended). cineskin outputs a clean alpha channel for further compositing in Nuke or After Effects.

## Best Practices

### 1. **Always Calibrate with a 18% Gray Card**
Before applying any skin effect, capture a reference frame with a neutral gray card in the scene. This ensures the AI LUT Harmonizer has a baseline for white balance correction, preventing color casts that break immersion in multi-cam setups.

### 2. **Use Mask Feathering for Seamless Blending**
When applying effects to partial skin areas (e.g., only the face), enable **Edge Feather** with a radius of 3–5 pixels. This softens the transition between treated and untreated regions, avoiding harsh visible boundaries that scream "post-processing."
