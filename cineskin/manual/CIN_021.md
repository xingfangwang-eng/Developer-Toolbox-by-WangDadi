# cineskin - CIN_021

## Overview

cineskin is a next-gen film-grade skin retouching and color grading engine designed for post-production pipelines. It bridges the gap between real-time preview and final-frame quality, leveraging AI-assisted masking and LUT interpolation to deliver consistent skin tones across complex lighting conditions.

## Hardcore Features

### 1. **Temporal Skin Consistency Engine**
cineskin analyzes skin regions frame-by-frame using a lightweight neural network, then applies temporal smoothing to prevent flickering or hue shifts during fast cuts or dynamic lighting. This ensures seamless skin tone transitions across entire sequences without manual keyframing.

### 2. **Multi-LUT Skin Mapping with Adaptive Curves**
Instead of applying a single LUT globally, cineskin maps up to 12 reference LUTs to specific skin zones (e.g., highlights, midtones, shadows) using adaptive curve interpolation. This allows for precise color correction that preserves natural skin texture while matching the director’s look.

### 3. **Subsurface Scattering Emulation via Pixel Displacement**
cineskin simulates realistic subsurface scattering by analyzing pixel displacement vectors in the YCbCr color space. This gives digital characters and actors a more organic, film-like appearance, especially under harsh key lights or extreme close-ups.

## How to Use

### 1. **Set Up the Temporal Skin Mask**
Open the clip in cineskin’s timeline view. Click the **“Analyze Skin”** button to trigger the neural mask generator. Adjust the **“Temporal Radius”** slider (default: 3 frames) to control how far the algorithm looks ahead/behind for smoothing. Enable **“Edge Preservation”** to avoid bleeding into non-skin regions.

### 2. **Apply Multi-LUT Skin Mapping**
Navigate to the **“LUT Manager”** panel. Drag your primary LUT into Slot A and secondary LUTs into Slots B–L. Use the **“Zone Picker”** to define skin zones based on luminance and saturation thresholds. cineskin will automatically interpolate between LUTs per zone. Fine-tune using the **“Blend Curve”** graph.

### 3. **Render with Subsurface Scattering**
In the export dialog, check **“Enable Subsurface Scattering Emulation”**. Set **“Displacement Strength”** (0.0–1.0) to control scattering intensity. For most footage, start at 0.3. Enable **“High-Frequency Detail Pass”** to retain skin pores and micro-texture. Export as DPX or ProRes 4444 for maximum fidelity.

## Best Practices

1. **Always calibrate your monitor to DCI-P3 before using cineskin’s LUT mapping.** The adaptive curves are designed around wide-gamut color spaces, and an uncalibrated display will result in inaccurate skin tone mapping.

2. **Use cineskin’s “Skin Confidence” overlay during grading.** This visual heatmap shows where the engine is most confident in its skin detection. If you see low-confidence areas (e.g., motion-blurred skin), manually paint a soft mask with the **“Brush Refine”** tool to avoid artifacts.
