# cineskin - CIN_017

## Overview

cineskin is a next-gen, AI-driven film skinning engine built for post-production pipelines. It decouples visual style from raw footage, allowing editors and colorists to apply cinematic looks without touching a LUT. Designed for DaVinci Resolve, Premiere Pro, and Nuke, cineskin bridges the gap between real-time preview and final-grade fidelity.

## What is cineskin?

cineskin is not another color grading plugin. It’s a **semantic film texture mapper** that analyzes scene content—skin tones, depth, motion vectors—and applies **dynamic film grain, halation, and color space transforms** per shot. Think of it as a programmable film stock emulator that adapts to your edit, not the other way around.

## Core Features

### 1. **Adaptive Film Stock Emulation with Scene-Aware Grain**
cineskin uses a neural network trained on 35mm, 16mm, and Super 8 stocks. Instead of overlaying static grain, it modulates grain intensity, size, and color bleed based on luminance, focus depth, and motion. Highlights get finer grain; shadows get chunkier, organic texture. No more “grain everywhere or nowhere.”

### 2. **Per-Shot Halation & Flare Modeling**
Mimics optical flare and halation from vintage anamorphic lenses—but with per-shot control. cineskin computes light scatter from actual highlight regions in your frame, not a global bloom. You can dial in lens-specific artifacts (e.g., Kowa Prominar vs. Cooke S4) without rendering passes.

### 3. **Real-Time Color Space Bridging (ACES / DWG / Rec.709)**
cineskin acts as a color pipeline adapter. It reads your timeline’s color space (ACEScct, DaVinci Wide Gamut, or custom) and maps film stock characteristics directly into that space. No need to convert to LogC or flatten your grade. It preserves your primary corrections while adding the film look.

## How to Use

### Step 1: Install and Load into Your NLE
Download the plugin for your platform (macOS/Linux/Win). In DaVinci Resolve, install via `Preferences > Color Management > OpenFX Plugins`. In Premiere Pro, copy the `.aex` file to your Common Plug-ins folder. Restart the app. Your new effect appears under “cineskin” in the effects panel.

### Step 2: Select Film Stock and Set Base Parameters
Drag cineskin onto a clip. In the control panel, choose a stock preset (e.g., “Kodak Vision3 500T”). Adjust `Exposure Compensation` and `Grain Scale` to match your scene’s ISO and lighting. Enable `Scene-Aware Grain` and `Adaptive Halation`. Preview in real-time—cineskin uses GPU acceleration for 4K at 24fps.

### Step 3: Fine-Tune Per Shot and Export
For critical shots, use the **Depth Mask** slider to limit film effects to specific focal planes (e.g., keep grain off the sky). Toggle `Flare Source` to “Highlights Only” to avoid washing out midtones. Once satisfied, render with `Preserve Grade` enabled. cineskin outputs a clean film look without altering your base color nodes.

## Best Practices

### 1. **Stack cineskin After Primary Grade, Before Output LUT**
Always place cineskin after your exposure and contrast adjustments, but before your final display LUT or Rec.709 conversion. This ensures the film emulation interacts with corrected colors, not raw sensor data. If you apply it before your grade, you’ll fight the grain and halation.

### 2. **Use Per-Clip Instances for Mixed Media**
If your timeline mixes digital cinema (ARRI, RED) with vintage footage (Hi8, DV), create separate cineskin instances per clip type. Set `Grain Base` to “Digital” for ARRI and “Analog” for Hi8. This prevents a uniform film look from breaking the visual consistency of mixed-source projects.
