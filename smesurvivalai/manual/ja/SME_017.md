### 2. Atomic Operations for Concurrency Control

In order to ensure the integrity and consistency of our data, we employ atomic operations to control concurrency. This involves using locks or other mechanisms to guarantee that multiple threads do not access shared resources simultaneously, thereby preventing data corruption or inconsistencies.

For instance, when updating a customer's purchasing intention (PI), we use an atomic operation to ensure that the update is either fully committed or rolled back, ensuring that the database remains in a consistent state.

### 3. Load Balancing and Circuit Breaker

To ensure high availability and fault tolerance, we implement a load balancing mechanism that distributes incoming requests across multiple instances of our application. This allows us to handle sudden spikes in traffic while minimizing the risk of any single instance becoming overwhelmed.

In addition, we use circuit breakers to detect when an instance is experiencing difficulties or errors, and automatically remove it from the load balancing pool until it recovers.
